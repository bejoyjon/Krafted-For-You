# Instagram Visual Design Spec

Brand: `Krafted For You`
Use: Instagram reels, carousel covers, testimonial cards, workshop promos, and story frames

## 1. Creative Direction

The page should feel:
- handcrafted
- thoughtful
- warm
- feminine without being overly delicate
- premium but still approachable

The visual language should move away from mixed one-off creatives and toward a system that looks intentional at grid level.

## 2. Working Brand Palette

Use this as the working palette until a formal brand guide is made.

| Role | Color | Hex |
|---|---|---|
| Primary maroon | Deep berry plum | `#6E1F47` |
| Secondary yellow | Warm saffron | `#F0B233` |
| Cream background | Soft ivory | `#F7F1E8` |
| Blush accent | Dusty rose | `#E8C9C9` |
| Green prop accent | Muted leaf | `#7C9363` |
| Dark text | Soft charcoal | `#2F2628` |

### Usage Rules
- Maroon for covers, headers, and high-contrast brand moments
- Cream for educational slides and elegant neutral backgrounds
- Yellow only as an accent, not full-slide body color
- Blush and green in props, illustrations, or small accents

## 3. Font System

These are Canva-friendly recommendations.

### Primary Headline Font
- `Cormorant Garamond SemiBold`
- Use for:
  - cover headlines
  - quote cards
  - elegant brand-led posts

### Secondary Support Font
- `Montserrat Medium`
- Use for:
  - subheads
  - body copy
  - CTA text
  - small labels

### Optional Accent Font
- `Montserrat SemiBold`
- Use sparingly for:
  - CTA buttons
  - category tags
  - slide numbering

## 4. Typography Rules

- Cover headline size should dominate the slide.
- Keep body copy short and well-spaced.
- Do not mix more than `2` font families in one design.
- Avoid all-caps for long lines.
- Use sentence case for a more premium feel.

## 5. Template System

Create these as reusable Canva templates.

### Template A: Founder Reel Cover
- Background: photo of founder or soft cream block
- Headline zone: upper-left or center-left
- Accent: small yellow line or brush stroke
- Footer: logo or handle

### Template B: Educational Carousel Cover
- Background: maroon or cream
- Headline: centered or left-aligned
- Supporting image: one hamper or tool visual
- Footer tag: `Save this`

### Template C: Testimonial Card
- Background: cream
- Quote text in maroon
- Small logo in one corner
- Optional product thumbnail

### Template D: Workshop Promo
- Background: real workshop photo
- Overlay block: maroon translucent panel
- Headline: cream serif
- CTA: `DM "WORKSHOP"`

### Template E: Product Use-Case Carousel
- Background: clean neutral
- Product image large
- Headline and one-line takeaway only

## 6. Grid Rules

For every set of 9 posts:
- at least `3` posts should feature the founder face
- at least `3` posts should show products clearly
- at least `2` posts should carry a strong text-led educational or proof headline
- at least `1` post should visibly promote workshops

Do not let more than `2` consecutive posts use the same visual treatment.

## 7. Photography Style

### Lighting
- Always prefer natural daylight
- Avoid harsh overhead tube lighting
- Use white foam board or a light-colored surface to bounce light if needed

### Styling
- Use ribbons, flowers, handwritten notes, textured paper, and neatly arranged products
- Keep table surfaces clean
- Use fewer props, not more
- Leave negative space around products

### Framing
- Combine:
  - overhead shots,
  - 45-degree styled product shots,
  - close-up detail shots,
  - medium founder shots

## 8. Reel Editing Style

- Hook should appear in the first `1-1.5` seconds
- Add subtitles in a consistent style
- Use quick cuts but not frantic cuts
- End with a clean CTA frame
- Use the same color grade on all founder reels

## 9. Subtitle Style

- Font: `Montserrat SemiBold`
- Text color: white or cream
- Background: semi-transparent maroon strip or shadow
- Placement: lower third, centered
- Max `2` lines at a time

## 10. Cover Design Rules

- Use one clear headline only
- Do not place more than `12` words on a cover
- Make sure the subject is legible at thumbnail size
- Keep one focal point
- Use the same logo placement across all covers

## 11. Story Design Rules

- Stories should look less formal than feed posts, but still branded
- Use:
  - cream or maroon text blocks
  - one sticker only when possible
  - candid workshop and BTS visuals
- Save the best story sequences into highlights

## 12. Highlight Redesign

Recommended highlight set:
- `Hampers`
- `Workshops`
- `Reviews`
- `BTS`
- `Corporate`

### Highlight Cover Style
- Cream background
- Maroon icon
- Simple line-art icon per highlight
- No tiny text

## 13. Canva Build Order

1. Create the color palette in brand kit
2. Add `Cormorant Garamond` and `Montserrat` as the working font pair
3. Build 5 reusable templates
4. Export covers for the first 9 planned posts in one sitting
5. Lock recurring elements:
   - logo position
   - subtitle style
   - CTA style
   - headline margins

## 14. What To Avoid

- Too many fonts in one post
- Busy collage layouts
- Low-light yellowish photos
- Paragraph-heavy text slides
- Random colors that break the maroon-cream-yellow system
- Generic motivational quotes with no business purpose
