# Weekly Agentic Operating Ritual

Date: `2026-05-23`

Use this once per week to keep Krafted For You operating as a small, agent-supported organization.

## Inputs

Export or update these trackers before the review:

- [customer_leads_template.csv](../../data/templates/customer_leads_template.csv)
- [orders_template.csv](../../data/templates/orders_template.csv)
- [content_calendar_template.csv](../../data/templates/content_calendar_template.csv)
- [content_kpis_template.csv](../../data/templates/content_kpis_template.csv)
- [vendors_template.csv](../../data/templates/vendors_template.csv)
- [workshops_template.csv](../../data/templates/workshops_template.csv)
- [customer_memory_template.csv](../../data/templates/customer_memory_template.csv)

## Monday Planning

Ask the Chief of Staff Agent:

```text
Using the latest trackers, create this week's operating queue for Krafted For You.

Separate:
- customer leads needing replies,
- quotes needing approval,
- orders at risk,
- vendor or material blockers,
- payment follow-ups,
- content due this week,
- workshop actions,
- customer delight or repeat-order opportunities,
- decisions needed from the founder.

Keep customer-facing messages as drafts only.
```

Founder approves:

- final quotes,
- delivery promises,
- high-risk customer responses,
- purchases,
- content posts.

## Wednesday Pipeline Check

Ask the Chief of Staff Agent:

```text
Review active leads and orders.

Find:
- leads older than 24 hours without a next action,
- quotes sent but not followed up,
- unpaid advances,
- orders with delivery dates inside 7 days,
- orders missing materials or vendor items,
- customer updates due.

Draft the smallest useful next action for each item.
```

## Weekend Learning Review

Ask the KPI and Learning Agent:

```text
Review this week's content, leads, orders, and workshops.

Tell me:
- what worked,
- what did not work,
- which content produced meaningful business signals,
- which inquiries converted or stalled,
- what repeated work should become a prompt, checklist, script, or automation,
- what to do next week.
```

## Definition Of A Good Week

A good week is not only a week with sales. It is a week where the business memory improved.

Minimum weekly outcomes:

- every active lead has a next action,
- every order has a visible status,
- every order risk is known,
- at least one content learning is captured,
- at least one reusable prompt, checklist, vendor note, product note, or customer memory is improved.
