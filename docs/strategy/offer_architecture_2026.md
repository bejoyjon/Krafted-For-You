# Krafted For You Offer Architecture 2026

Date: `2026-05-24`

Scope: first-pass offer architecture focused on `Customer Emotion Intelligence` and `Curated Option Architecture`.

## 1. Offer Architecture Goal

Make Krafted For You easier to buy from without making it feel generic.

The offer architecture should guide the customer from:

`I need a special gift but I do not know exactly what to do`

to:

`Here are 2-3 thoughtful, premium directions that fit the recipient, occasion, budget, and timeline.`

## 2. Primary Offer Lanes For First Build

Start with three lanes before expanding:

1. Bespoke Premium Hampers
2. Signature Handmade Keepsakes
3. Curated Occasion Gifting

Workshops and corporate gifting remain strategic lanes, but the first capability build should focus on personal gifting because it is closest to current word-of-mouth demand.

## 3. Lane-Level Tactical Plan

| Offer lane | Emotional intelligence task | Curated option task | First output |
|---|---|---|---|
| Bespoke Premium Hampers | Understand relationship, recipient personality, and desired feeling | Create 2-3 hamper concepts by emotion, style, and budget | Hamper concept proposal template |
| Signature Handmade Keepsakes | Understand what memory or identity the keepsake should preserve | Offer controlled customization levers | Keepsake customization menu |
| Curated Occasion Gifting | Define the emotional job of each occasion | Build repeatable good-better-best paths | Occasion playbook |

## 4. Suggested First Occasion Playbooks

Start with:

1. Birthday
2. Anniversary
3. Farewell or gratitude

These are frequent, emotionally clear, and suitable for both hampers and handmade keepsakes.

## 5. Option Path Template

Every option path should include:

- concept name,
- emotional intent,
- best-fit recipient type,
- likely inclusions,
- handmade element,
- sourced element,
- personalization lever,
- budget band,
- minimum lead time,
- risk or dependency,
- why this option fits.

## 6. Example Option Structure

For one qualified inquiry, propose:

| Option | Role |
|---|---|
| Option A: Safe refined choice | Elegant, feasible, lower complexity |
| Option B: Most personal choice | Strongest emotional fit, medium complexity |
| Option C: Premium statement choice | Highest impact, higher budget or lead time |

This avoids a flat menu and helps customers choose by intent.

## 7. Challenges And Mitigations

| Challenge | Risk | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|
| Too many choices | Customer delays decision | Limit to 2-3 paths |
| Too much customization | Founder time explodes | Define customization levers per lane |
| Too generic | Brand loses bespoke value | Every option must include emotional intent and personalization |
| Too vague | Quote cannot be prepared | Require budget, date, recipient, occasion, desired feeling |
| Too premium too early | Customer may hesitate | Give good-better-best paths with clear reasoning |

## 8. Data Inputs Needed

- past 20-30 orders,
- top customer occasions,
- photos of completed work,
- common budget ranges,
- common sourcing categories,
- common customer questions,
- difficult order examples,
- founder notes on what made an order beautiful or stressful.

## 9. Final Output

The finished first version should include:

- 3 occasion playbooks,
- 3 hamper concept patterns,
- 3 keepsake customization menus,
- proposal template,
- option path template,
- fit and refusal rules,
- initial price-band assumptions.

## 10. Feedback Loop

Track for every proposal:

- which option was chosen,
- which option was ignored,
- what confused the customer,
- what they asked to change,
- whether the budget was acceptable,
- whether the proposal converted.

Review every month and simplify the offer architecture.
