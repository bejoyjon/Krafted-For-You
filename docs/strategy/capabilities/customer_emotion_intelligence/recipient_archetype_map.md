# Recipient Archetype Map

Purpose: create reusable starting points for understanding recipients without stereotyping them.

## Template

| Archetype | What they value | Gift direction | Useful questions | Avoid |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Quiet Sentimentalist | Meaning, memory, subtle personalization | Notes, keepsakes, personal colors, memory-linked objects | What memory would they quietly treasure? | Loud personalization |
| Refined Indulger | Premium sensory experience | Fragrance, treats, textures, elegant packaging | What feels indulgent but tasteful to them? | Cheap fillers |
| Practical Aesthete | Useful objects that look beautiful | Decor, desk, home, daily-use items | What object would improve their daily ritual? | Purely decorative clutter |
| Creative Explorer | Making, learning, artistic process | Workshop, DIY kit, handmade material story | What would they enjoy making or learning? | Generic finished product only |
| Status-Conscious Professional | Presentation, restraint, polish | Minimal premium hamper, elegant packaging | What should the gift signal professionally? | Overly cute or cluttered gifts |

## How To Fill

Start with these five, then update from reconstructed orders. Add an archetype only if at least three examples support it or if the founder recognizes it as a recurring customer pattern.

## How To Analyze

Review which archetypes:

- appear most often,
- convert into orders,
- produce strong customer reactions,
- create sourcing or design difficulty.

Remove archetypes that do not help option generation.
