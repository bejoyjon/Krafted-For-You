# Krafted For You Brand Strategy 2026

Date: `2026-05-24`

Scope: first-pass brand strategy focused on building `Customer Emotion Intelligence` and `Curated Option Architecture`.

## 1. Brand Role

Krafted For You should become the brand customers trust when they want a gift to feel personal, refined, and emotionally accurate, but they do not want to carry the creative burden themselves.

The brand should not simply say:

`We make custom gifts.`

It should communicate:

`We understand what you want the recipient to feel, then guide you toward a beautifully crafted gift that expresses it.`

## 2. Success Definition

| Success dimension | What success looks like | How to achieve it |
|---|---|---|
| Brand clarity | Customers can explain Krafted For You in one sentence | Repeat the same positioning across customer conversations, digital surfaces, and content |
| Emotional precision | Customers feel the gift idea reflects the recipient and relationship | Build a standard emotional brief and recipient profile system |
| Guided premium experience | Customers do not feel overwhelmed by choices | Offer 2-3 curated paths instead of open-ended customization |
| Sophisticated perception | Gifts and communication feel tasteful, not amateur or generic | Define visual, language, packaging, and proposal standards |
| Trust | Customers believe Krafted can handle important occasions | Show proof through brief-to-gift stories, testimonials, and reliable process cues |

## 3. Brand Moat

The moat is the combination of:

1. emotional understanding,
2. premium taste,
3. guided decision-making,
4. bespoke craft,
5. reliability.

Most bespoke artists can show craft. Fewer can guide a customer from vague emotional intention to a refined, feasible, on-time gift.

## 4. Tactical Approaches

| Approach | Benefit | Challenge | How to overcome |
|---|---|---|---|
| Emotional brief before design | Makes gifts more meaningful | Customers may not know how to answer emotional questions | Use simple prompts: relationship, occasion, recipient personality, desired feeling |
| Recipient archetypes | Speeds interpretation of vague briefs | Archetypes can become stereotyped | Treat archetypes as starting points, not labels |
| Curated option paths | Reduces decision fatigue | Customers may expect unlimited customization | Explain that curated options protect taste, quality, and delivery |
| Brand language templates | Creates consistency | Risk of sounding scripted | Keep templates modular and edit every final message manually |
| Proof stories | Makes word of mouth visible | Past orders may lack documentation | Start with reconstructed stories from memory, then capture proof going forward |

## 5. Data Sources

Use these data sources before doing any external research:

| Data source | What to extract |
|---|---|
| Past customer conversations | Common occasions, emotional language, budget range, confusion points |
| Founder memory | Best orders, difficult orders, recurring customer types, why people bought |
| Past photos and videos | Visual proof, product types, aesthetic patterns |
| Customer testimonials or reactions | Words customers use to describe value |
| Referral history | Who referred whom and why |
| Instagram history | Existing visual assets and audience signals, but not as the assumed strategy center |
| Workshop history | Participant motivations and proof of craft trust |

## 6. Final Brand Outputs

The first brand outputs should be:

1. one-sentence positioning,
2. brand promise,
3. what-we-do and what-we-do-not-do list,
4. emotional brief template,
5. recipient archetype starter set,
6. tone-of-voice guide,
7. proof-story format,
8. offer-lane descriptions written in customer language.

## 7. Progress Tracking

| Metric | Target signal |
|---|---|
| Time to explain brand | A new customer understands the offer in under 1 minute |
| Brief completeness | 80% of serious inquiries have occasion, recipient, relationship, budget, date, desired feeling |
| Quote clarity | 80% of proposals use 2-3 curated option paths |
| Customer language | Customers repeat words like thoughtful, personal, refined, beautiful, easy |
| Proof library | At least 1 reusable story captured per completed strong order |

## 8. Feedback Loop

Every month, review:

1. Which phrases made customers respond positively?
2. Which emotional brief questions produced useful answers?
3. Which questions confused customers?
4. Which option paths converted?
5. Which proof stories made people inquire?

Revise the brand language and emotional brief monthly until the conversion flow feels clear and repeatable.
