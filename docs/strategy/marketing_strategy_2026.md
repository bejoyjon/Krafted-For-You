# Krafted For You Marketing Strategy 2026

Date: `2026-05-24`

Scope: marketing strategy for the first capability build.

## 1. Marketing Goal

Convert word-of-mouth trust into visible market memory.

The market should remember:

`Krafted For You helps people give gifts that feel deeply personal, refined, and thoughtfully guided.`

## 2. Success Definition

| Success area | What success looks like |
|---|---|
| Brand recall | People associate Krafted with thoughtful premium gifting |
| Proof | Completed gifts produce visible stories |
| Inquiry quality | More inquiries arrive with clearer occasion and recipient context |
| Guided choice | Customers understand they will receive curated options |
| Word-of-mouth amplification | Referrals become visible and repeatable |

## 3. Content Themes For The First Build

| Theme | Capability supported | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Brief to gift | Customer Emotion Intelligence | "She wanted a farewell gift that felt grateful, not formal." |
| 3 ways to gift for an occasion | Curated Option Architecture | 3 birthday gift directions by recipient type |
| Recipient personality gifting | Customer Emotion Intelligence | Gift ideas for someone sentimental, minimalist, indulgent |
| Process and detail | Aesthetic standards | Why a certain material, note, or packaging choice was used |
| Customer reaction | Proof engine | Testimonial or recipient response |

## 4. Tactical Challenges

| Challenge | Mitigation |
|---|---|
| Old orders lack documentation | Reconstruct stories from founder memory and available photos |
| Content becomes product display | Always include customer problem, recipient, or desired feeling |
| Too much public detail violates privacy | Use anonymized stories and permission-based testimonials |
| Posting feels disconnected from sales | Tie every content piece to an offer lane or inquiry prompt |

## 5. Data Sources

- old order photos,
- customer reactions,
- founder notes,
- referral stories,
- common customer questions,
- workshop photos and participant feedback,
- proposal outcomes.

## 6. Final Outputs

- 10 brief-to-gift story notes,
- 5 recipient archetype content prompts,
- 3 occasion content series,
- proof capture checklist,
- testimonial request draft,
- content scorecard.

## 7. Feedback Loop

Track:

- which stories lead to inquiries,
- which content receives saves or shares,
- which recipient archetypes resonate,
- which offer lane the inquiry maps to,
- whether inquiry quality improves after content.

Monthly, revise content pillars based on inquiry quality, not vanity metrics alone.

## 8. Tie To First-Year Priorities

| Priority | Marketing contribution |
|---|---|
| Become Legible | Repeated stories clarify what the brand does |
| Productize Without Losing Bespoke | Option-path content teaches customers how to buy |
| Build A Proof Library | Marketing depends on proof capture from each order |
| Build Vendor Memory | Sourcing stories can show thoughtful curation |
| Build Agent-Readable Business Memory | Content performance and inquiry outcomes become learning data |
